WHAT IS AN EXOPLANET?

Description
An exoplanet is any planet beyond our solar system. Most orbit other stars, but free-floating exoplanets, called rogue planets, orbit the galactic center and are untethered to any star.
Types of Exoplanets
Rocky planets, Super-Earth, Ocean planets and Desert planets, Gas Giant, Hot Jupiter, Rogue planets.
Methods of Exoplanet Detection
Introduction, Direct Imaging Method, Astrometry Method, Radial Velocity Method, Transit event Observation Method
Number of Exoplanets
These are lists of exoplanets. As of 1 December 2022, there are 5,284 confirmed exoplanets in 3,899 planetary systems, with 847 systems having more than one planet.
